# 1Password Software Development Career Ladder
[Level Progression](index.md) | [Competencies](competencies.md)

## Competencies
Mastery of a competency is cumulative. At each successive level, software developers are expected to retain the skills and characteristics required at earlier levels.

The competencies are:

* Delivery
* Domain Expertise
* Problem Solving
* Communication
* Leadership

### Delivery
**Example Skills:** Scoping and prioritization, testing and monitoring, shipping to production, initiative

    <thead>
        <th style="width:20%;">Beginner</th>
        <th style="width:20%;">Intermediate</th>
        <th style="width:20%;">Advanced</th>
        <th style="width:20%;">Expert</th>
        <th style="width:20%;">Leading Expert</th>
    </thead>
    <tbody>
        <tr>
            <td valign="top">
                <ul>
                    <li>You understand the scope of work before taking on tasks.</li>
                    <li>You test your work following relevant examples.</li>
                    <li>You take ownership of your work after it’s released and quickly address issues as they arise.</li>
                    <li>You collect and incorporate feedback (such as code reviews) to ship your work.</li>
                    <li>You follow your team’s best practices to bring your work to customers.</li>
                </ul>
            </td>
            <td valign="top">
                <ul>
					<li>You solve problems within a reasonable timeframe to ship updates to customers.</li>
					<li>Your changes are well tested to ensure their quality and production readiness both before and after release. This can take the form of unit, integration, and user interface tests.</li>
                    <li>You consider and build for many different use cases but avoid over-engineering and premature optimization.</li>
					<li>You break your work up into manageable, reviewable chunks that are steps along the way to the final feature, improvement, or bug fix.</li>
                    <li>You identify important tradeoffs and review them with your team.</li>
                    <li>You value clear code over clever solutions.</li>
                </ul>
            </td>
            <td valign="top">
                <ul>
					<li>You collaborate with your team to plan the work ahead, including estimates of effort and risk, to get changes out to customers.</li>
					<li>You communicate with your team and take responsibility for your work, both when it’s on track and when it isn’t.</li>
					<li>Beyond tests, you are using additional tools such as profilers and analysis tools to ensure your changes are of a high quality before shipping them to customers.</li>
					<li>You watch for signs of trouble after shipping your changes to customers. Places to monitor include social media, support forums and email support, and internal communication channels.</li>
                    <li>You understand how the work you and others are doing affects other projects and strive to ensure minimal friction between them when shipping them to customers.</li>
                    <li>You come up with solutions that may deviate from standard practices in order to unblock the work of you and your teammates.</li>
					<li>You work effectively at a variety of speeds. You are able to move quickly when developing low-risk solutions under tight timelines, but are also able to move at a more deliberate, cautious pace when the situation calls for it.</li>
					<li>You understand the tasks that matter most and work on those tasks, even if your capabilities far outweigh the challenge.</li>
                </ul>
            </td>
            <td valign="top">
                <ul>
                    <li>You bring together the work of different teams into a cohesive, achievable plan that aligns with the goals of the company and ships to customers in a reasonable amount of time.</li>
					<li>You come up with new and creative ways to ensure the quality of the changes you are shipping.</li>
                    <li>You are aware of and understand the work being done by other teams and make plans and decisions to minimize surprises and conflicts down the road.</li>
					<li>You create widely used technical solutions to measure the quality of the software being developed at the company.</li>
                </ul>
            </td>
            <td valign="top">
                <ul>
                    <li>You identify and eliminate widely-felt barriers to productivity, finding ways to make everyone more effective at shipping updates to our customers.</li>
                    <li>You create tools to help software developers ship better products, not just within the company, but across the software industry as a whole.</li>
                    <li>You come up with solutions that continue to work without your involvement and don’t require you to maintain them.</li>
                </ul>
            </td>
        </tr>
    </tbody>
</table>

### Domain Expertise
**Example Skills:** Knowledge of your discipline (e.g. user interface development, billing systems, machine learning, database design, app and web extension development, API design), language, and tools.
<table>
    <thead>
        <th style="width:20%;">Beginner</th>
        <th style="width:20%;">Intermediate</th>
        <th style="width:20%;">Advanced</th>
        <th style="width:20%;">Expert</th>
        <th style="width:20%;">Leading Expert</th>
    </thead>
    <tbody>
        <tr>
            <td valign="top">
                <ul>
                    <li>You ask questions to support your own continuous learning.</li>
                    <li>You learn independently and from those around you.</li>
                    <li>You are always eager to learn more.</li>
                </ul>
            </td>
            <td valign="top">
                <ul>
                    <li>You know how to do your job well and follow the direction in which your development community is heading.</li>
                    <li>You are able to learn and expand your expertise with and without help.</li>
                    <li>You’re proficient with the software development tools you need to get your job done.</li>
                    <li>You know your product inside and out and also have some knowledge of other products and projects.</li>
                    <li>You understand why your work is important to our customers and how it relates to your team’s goals.</li>
                </ul>
            </td>
            <td valign="top">
                <ul>
					<li>You are able to use your specialized knowledge to ensure that projects succeed.</li>
                    <li>You know what tools are available and useful for the problems you are trying to solve. You also know how they work and are able to teach your teammates to use them as well.</li>
                    <li>You have strong, well-founded opinions about how to build software, but you’re adaptable and open to new ideas.</li>
                    <li>You closely follow the trends in your software development community and understand how to apply them to your work as appropriate.</li>
                    <li>You share your knowledge with your teammates. You’re able to explain things about which you know a great deal to those who do not in a way that is understandable and helpful.</li>
                </ul>
            </td>
            <td valign="top">
                <ul>
					<li>You are able to use your expertise to raise the level of software development at the company.</li>
                    <li>You actively share your expertise and look for opportunities to teach others.</li>
                    <li>You take concepts and technologies from your software development community and use them to solve important problems.</li>
                    <li>Work you have done or that is based on your expertise has consistently been successful. Your expertise helps projects and teams achieve and exceed their goals.</li>
                </ul>
            </td>
            <td valign="top">
                <ul>
                    <li>Your contributions to the field (e.g. talks, open source projects, research papers) have meaningful impact beyond the company.</li>
                    <li>Your expertise helps the company achieve and exceed its goals.</li>
                    <li>You use your expertise to come up with solutions to long-standing or seemingly intractable problems.</li>
                    <li>You contribute to the advancement of the wider software industry in your area of expertise.</li>
                </ul>
            </td>
        </tr>
    </tbody>
</table>

### Problem Solving
**Example Skills:** Architecture &amp; design patterns, breaking down large problems, critical thinking, analytical skills, creativity, and decision making
<table>
    <thead>
        <th style="width:20%;">Beginner</th>
        <th style="width:20%;">Intermediate</th>
        <th style="width:20%;">Advanced</th>
        <th style="width:20%;">Expert</th>
        <th style="width:20%;">Leading Expert</th>
    </thead>
    <tbody>
        <tr>
            <td valign="top">
                <ul>
                    <li>You’re able to debug your own code easily, and you can debug unfamiliar code with some effort.</li>
                    <li>You recognize when you don’t yet have the experience or knowledge to solve a problem and reach out to others for help and guidance.</li>
                    <li>You are beginning to understand which tools are relevant to your work and how to use them.</li>
                    <li>You prepare to work on new tasks by planning an approach ahead of time and confirming it is well thought out.</li>
                    <li>You approach each new task as an opportunity to learn and continually apply what you’ve learned to each new challenge.</li>
                </ul>
            </td>
            <td valign="top">
                <ul>
                    <li>You identify and eliminate repetitive problems rather than working around them. For example, having the continuous integration service create builds for QA to test instead of having QA build each branch themselves.</li>
                    <li>You recognize your own mistakes, use them as a learning and teaching opportunity, and change your approach to avoid repeating those mistakes.</li>
                    <li>You use new technologies, patterns, and the domain expertise of fellow software developers to increase your own skills.</li>
                    <li>You are learning to break down large problems into smaller, more manageable ones.</li>
                    <li>You have strong debugging skills, allowing you to determine the source of issues in unfamiliar code.</li>
                </ul>
            </td>
            <td valign="top">
                <ul>
                    <li>You rely on your problem solving experience to identify and solve issues with ease even when they are not in your area of expertise.</li>
                    <li>You plan out your work in advance to identify edge cases and minimize complications down the road.</li>
                    <li>You come up with creative solutions to difficult problems using rapid prototyping and proofs of concept.</li>
                    <li>You reflect on solutions you have created, measure their impact, and use that information to conceive of and optimize future work.</li>
                    <li>You break down complex problems into smaller, more manageable ones, and help others to do the same.</li>
                    <li>You are adept at making decisions that involve a significant number of factors and have broad implications.</li>
					<li>You are able to use future roadmaps, customer research, and relevant software development principles to inform the decisions you make, assuring your solutions are built for the long term.</li>
                </ul>
            </td>
            <td valign="top">
                <ul>
                    <li>You use your debugging skills to solve urgent issues in areas of the codebase with which you are unfamiliar and are capable of providing support across a broad range of issues.</li>
					<li>Your work is some of the best at the company in terms of performance, stability, and maintainability.</li>
                    <li>You create solutions that do not rely on you personally to maintain, debug, and improve.</li>
                    <li>Your architectural decisions are based on a strong understanding of the tradeoffs of industry best practices.</li>
                    <li>You are able to look ahead to identify areas of greatest need for a particular project, and turn this into actionable milestones.</li>
                </ul>
            </td>
            <td valign="top">
                <ul>
                    <li>You create architecture that gets broad acceptance and adoption within the compay, and which provides continual benefit with little support or overhead for a very long lifespan.</li>
                    <li>You provide actionable direction to teams in the face of complex problems.</li>
                    <li>You create new technologies that solve industry-wide problems.</li>
                </ul>
            </td>
        </tr>
    </tbody>
</table>

### Communication
**Example Skills:** Documentation, Collaboration, Relationship-building, Interviewing, Listening, Empathy

    <thead>
        <th style="width:20%;">Beginner</th>
        <th style="width:20%;">Intermediate</th>
        <th style="width:20%;">Advanced</th>
        <th style="width:20%;">Expert</th>
        <th style="width:20%;">Leading Expert</th>
    </thead>
    <tbody>
        <tr>
            <td valign="top">
                <ul>
                    <li>You are able to clearly articulate questions.</li>
                    <li>You are learning to collaborate with team members in various roles (e.g. other software developers, product managers, designers)</li>
                    <li>You know when to keep digging and when to ask for help. You know when you are stuck and need to be unblocked.</li>
                    <li>When you learn something new, or figure out the solution to a problem, you write documentation that helps others when they encounter similar issues.</li>
                </ul>
            </td>
            <td valign="top">
                <ul>
                    <li>You regularly write documentation related to your work that is clear and easy to follow.</li>
                    <li>You are able to take in vague requirements and ask the right questions to ensure they are clarified.</li>
                    <li>You have a sense for when and how to appropriately and effectively offer feedback.</li>
                    <li>You seek out and receive constructive criticism well.</li>
                    <li>You listen when others are speaking and make space for your colleagues to share their thoughts.</li>
                    <li>You actively participate in interviewing and provide thoughtful feedback.</li>
                </ul>
            </td>
            <td valign="top">
                <ul>
                    <li>You collaborate well with team members as both a mentor and a mentee.</li>
                    <li>Your written communication is concise and clear. You document not only the narrow projects you are working on but also larger initiatives, and you make sure the right people have the right context.</li>
                    <li>You collaborate effectively with your teammates and with others outside your team. You encourage collaboration among others.</li>
                    <li>You quickly extract core issues from discussions and meetings. Your presence makes meetings more productive.</li>
                    <li>You excel at giving and receiving constructive feedback; you both give and receive feedback clearly and with empathy.</li>
                    <li>You explain technical concepts clearly and with patience, and adjust your communication based on the audience. You offer help when asked, but you also know when to step back and let others work things out on their own.</li>
                    <li>You’re an attentive and empathetic listener. You ensure that everyone you’re working with gets a chance to share their thoughts and that they’re heard.</li>
                </ul>
            </td>
            <td valign="top">
                <ul>
                    <li>You make yourself accountable for the documentation of very large projects, and are able to coordinate with others to ensure documentation gets written.</li>
                    <li>Your documentation ensures that architectures can be maintained or built upon by people who were not originally involved in building them.</li>
                    <li>You help disparate groups of people collaborate, identify common goals, and reach consensus. Your facilitation skills keep conversations productive and respectful.</li>
                    <li>Others turn to you for help giving difficult feedback. Others often seek out your advice on matters involving communication.</li>
                    <li>You adapt your language to meet the needs of various levels of technical and non-technical audiences. Others seek you out because you’re approachable, respectful, and they know they will learn from you.</li>
                    <li>You not only practice mindful communication and active listening in your interactions but encourage these practices in others.</li>
                </ul>
            </td>
            <td valign="top">
                <ul>
                    <li>You’ve made significant changes to how the company handles documentation, facilitation, or collaboration in way that has made the entire company better.</li>
                    <li>You create spaces and channels that improve cross-team and inter-departmental communication.</li>
                    <li>Others often turn to you to make and communicate difficult decisions.</li>
                    <li>Your presence and example has a strong, positive influence on the working environment at the company.</li>
                </ul>
            </td>
        </tr>
    </tbody>
</table>

### Leadership
**Example Skills:** Accountability, responsibility, mentorship, making hard decisions, setting an example
<table>
    <thead>
        <th style="width:20%;">Beginner</th>
        <th style="width:20%;">Intermediate</th>
        <th style="width:20%;">Advanced</th>
        <th style="width:20%;">Expert</th>
        <th style="width:20%;">Leading Expert</th>
    </thead>
    <tbody>
        <tr>
            <td valign="top">
                <ul>
                    <li>You accept direction and guidance and are learning what traits are valuable in a leader.</li>
                    <li>You are transparent about what you do not understand and need help with, and communicate this to your manager.</li>
                    <li>You seek feedback often with an eye towards improving.</li>
                </ul>
            </td>
            <td valign="top">
                <ul>
                    <li>You identify problems and propose solutions to them.</li>
                    <li>You are self-motivated in tackling your team’s projects.</li>
                    <li>You seek feedback often and take actionable steps to respond to the feedback.</li>
                    <li>You help create a safe environment for others to learn and grow as software developers.</li>
                    <li>You take on small roles that involve direct leadership (being a mentor, running a working group, etc).</li>
                </ul>
            </td>
            <td valign="top">
                <ul>
                    <li>You take action when there is a problem without a clear owner, and you don’t shy away from being the accountable party.</li>
                    <li>You inspire and motivate others to tackle projects.</li>
                    <li>You are generous with your time and foster an environment where people feel comfortable asking questions.</li>
                    <li>You are decisive and your technical depth is an asset to the decision making process.</li>
                    <li>You turn ideas and opportunities into plans and goals that can be picked up by your teammates or other teams.</li>
                    <li>You often take on roles involving direct leadership (for example, coordinating a long-term project or initiative).</li>
                </ul>
            </td>
            <td valign="top">
                <ul>
                    <li>You have successfully changed how the company functions to meet a critical need.</li>
                    <li>You have a large internal network and engage with other leaders to solve problems (technical, organizational, or social). You have developed strong credibility throughout the company.</li>
                    <li>You improve the entire software development organization by teaching others and sharing your knowledge. You create opportunities for others to showcase and develop their skills.</li>
                </ul>
            </td>
            <td valign="top">
                <ul>
                    <li>You have successfully changed how the company functions in a way that has moved both 1Password forward and established a best practice in the software development industry.</li>
                    <li>You regularly represent 1Password publicly, e.g. via blog posts, giving talks.</li>
                    <li>Your technical leadership is cited by many as a significantly positive aspect of working at the company.</li>
                    <li>You make hard decisions in the face of uncertainty.</li>
                </ul>
            </td>
        </tr>
    </tbody>
</table>
